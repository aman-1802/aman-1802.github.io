AI-Driven Workflow

AURELIUS is a fictional luxury watch brand created entirely from scratch — brand name, identity, product visuals, and the commercial itself. Every frame, every shot, every line of copy was built using AI tools. No camera. No studio. No real footage.

"147 components. One watch." — The line that anchored the entire creative direction.
Behind the Build
The Production Pipeline
01
Claude
Concept, Design & Storyboarding
Started with multiple brainstorming sessions on Claude to design a hero watch that would stay visually consistent across all 13 frames. Then asked Claude to write a complete 13-frame story arc — scenes covering the overall look, close-up shots, the dustproof and waterproof features, and the classic 7-layer explosion view showing the internal architecture. Claude also generated the final prompts for each frame. The goal: a royal, premium luxury aesthetic throughout.
02
ChatGPT Image 2.0
Image Generation — 13 Stills
Switched to ChatGPT and used it purely for image creation. Copy-pasted each of Claude's prompts, tweaking them wherever needed to get the right output. Generated all 13 stills this way — one per scene in the storyboard. Researched real luxury brand websites for visual inspiration before finalising the aesthetic direction.
03
Gemini — VEO 3.1
Animation — Bringing the Stills to Life
Used Gemini's VEO 3.1 model to animate each of the 13 still images into 4–5 second video clips — which would combine to a near-60-second final video. Gemini also handled the audio generation for the commercial.
04
VN Video Editor
Manual Editing — The Human Touch
AI could not stitch the clips together in a production-ready way — every attempt produced something absurd. So this step was done manually. Imported all 13 clips into VN, made the necessary cuts, synced audio, adjusted frame timings, and edited transitions between scenes. This is where the final polish happened.

Key Learnings
1
AI image generation is excellent — but iterations are non-negotiable. ChatGPT's image model is nearly perfect, but accepting the first draft is rarely the right call. The model occasionally hallucinates. Multiple rounds of iteration are what separate a good output from a great one.
2
AI video works best at short clip lengths (5–7 seconds). For longer formats, quality drops and coherence breaks. The smarter approach is to animate individual scenes and assemble them manually rather than asking AI to produce a full-length video in one shot.
3
Consistency across frames is no longer a major challenge. Both ChatGPT and Gemini maintained the same watch design and environment throughout the entire video — something that used to be a serious limitation in earlier models. This was the most impressive observation from the whole process.
4
Planning is the single most critical step. The brainstorming and storyboarding session with Claude at the very start defined everything that followed. Without it, the final result would have looked completely different — and far less cohesive.

AI has improved dramatically in video and image creation — and it will only get faster from here. But the right objective has always been the same: use AI as an intelligent assistant, hand it the tasks that are mundane and repetitive, and always keep a human in the loop to monitor and refine what it delivers.
